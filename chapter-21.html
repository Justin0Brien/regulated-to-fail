FIG 20.0: ANTITRUST_RISK
The Legal Deep Freeze

The Competition Chill

"When Being Sensible Looks Like Collusion"

Analysis of Chapter 20: How competition law treats universities as economic rivals, freezing the very collaboration needed to solve the sector's crisis.

The Two Faces of a University

The Civic Self

Public Perception

  • Collaborates for public good
  • Avoids waste & duplication
  • Plans regionally

The Legal Self

"The Undertaking"

  • Competes for customers (students)
  • Must not coordinate behaviour
  • Sharing plans = "Information Exchange"

"To the law, a university discussing course closures with a neighbour looks suspiciously like a supermarket fixing prices."

The Coordination Paradox

Caught between Policy and Law

The Government Says: "Rationalise Provision"

Stop duplicating courses. Share resources.

THE COLLISION

You cannot rationalise without agreeing who does what.

The Law Says: "Do Not Allocate Markets"

Agreeing "You do History, I'll do Geog" is illegal.

The "Chill" Spectrum

What can universities actually talk about?

SAFE (Mostly) Buying toilet paper together. Shared IT systems. Research equipment.
GREY ZONE (The Chill) Staff retention plans. Student support models. Benchmarking data.
RADIOACTIVE Tuition fees (discounts). Closing courses to help a neighbour. Wages.

"The chill comes because the Grey Zone is where all the useful strategy happens."

The Merger Chill

Two Weak Universities

Agree to merge to survive.

CMA JURISDICTION
  • • No mandatory notification (sounds good...)
  • • BUT CMA can "call in" any deal.
  • • Investigation freezes integration.
  • • Result: Uncertainty kills the deal.

The "Do Nothing" Option

Easier to drift into failure than risk intervention.
