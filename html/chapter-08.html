Retail Rules vs Civic Reality

The Regulation Mismatch

"Planning for Exits that Cannot Happen"

Analysis of Chapter 7: How the HERA 2017 settlement treats universities as shops that can fail safely, ignoring their role as interconnected civic infrastructure.

The Retail Theory

The Regulatory Assumption

  • Consumer Contract: Students are customers buying a product.
  • Market Exit: Failure is acceptable and disciplined ("Orderly Exit").
  • Responsiveness: Provision should change fast to meet demand.

The Civic Reality

The Operational Truth

  • Anchor Institution: Failure destroys local economies & pipelines.
  • Contagion: Exit infects partners, lenders, and sector confidence.
  • Contract Lock: CMA rules make quick "product changes" legally risky.
