FIG 29.0: POLICY_STABILISERS
Technical Fixes, Strategic Impact

The Stabiliser Protocol

"Small changes that dampen the shocks."

Analysis of Chapter 29: The handful of technical policy adjustments—Indexation, SAR, Sandbox—that would stabilise the sector without a new Act of Parliament.

Stabiliser 1: Automatic Indexation

Current: "Political Pain"
  • Inflation happens.
  • Minister must choose to raise fee.
  • Result: Freeze (Real-terms cut).
Proposed: "Automatic"
  • Inflation happens.
  • Formula applies uplift.
  • Result: Stability (No headline).

"It removes the need for a Minister to expend political capital just to stand still."

Stabiliser 2: Honest Research Funding

"Fund what you require."

Current: Low Price, High Volume 80% fEC

Requires Cross-Subsidy

Proposed: True Price, Lower Volume 100% fEC

Self-Sustaining

The trade-off: If the budget is fixed, paying the true cost means funding fewer projects.

Stabiliser 3: Orderly Exit (SAR)

A Special Administration Regime for HE.

Current Law (Insolvency Act) Administrator's duty is to Creditors. Students are unsecured. Asset fire-sale.
Proposed (SAR) Administrator's duty is to Learner Continuity. Protected teach-out. Managed transfer.

SAFE ZONE
Micro-creds Light-touch QA
2-Year Degrees Flexible Funding
Online Only Data Pilot

"Suspend the full weight of regulation for specific, low-risk pilots to prove they work."
